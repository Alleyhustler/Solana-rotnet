Black Bean Burgers

Why This Recipe?

Black bean burgers are not vegetarian hamburgers, though they are vegetarian. Their crispy outside and soft inside is more like a falafel or croquette on a bun. If you want a hamburger, make one of those. If you want something else on a bun, try this. This recipe makes 3 patties.

Ingredients

Patties

  • ¾ cup dry black beans
  • ⅔ cup bread crumbs
  • ½ bell pepper, finely diced
  • 1 egg
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • salt, pepper, and MSG to taste
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil

Possible Toppings

  • cheddar cheese
  • lettuce
  • tomatoes
  • pickles
  • avocado/guacamole
  • sautéed onions and/or mushrooms
  • muffuletta spread
  • condiments

Instructions

  1. Add ¾ cup dry black beans to a pressure cooker with a pinch of salt. Cover with water and cook for 25 minutes.
  2. Strain beans and add to a large mixing bowl with all other patty ingredients except cooking oil.
  3. Mash with a fork to combine until mixture firmly holds together.
  4. Form mixture into patties of desired size and shape. I enjoy 140 g patties.
  5. In a large skillet, cook patties in oil over medium heat until lightly browned, 4-6 minutes per side.
  6. If adding cheese, apply to patties in pan just before done. Add 1-2 tbsp of water and cover to steam melt the cheese.
  7. Serve on buns and top with desired fixings - don't go crazy, but have fun!
